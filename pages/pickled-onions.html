Pickled onions are one of the greatest, if not the absolute greatest topping ever (in my humble opinion) I am yet to find a dish to which you cannot add them, or eat them as side dish. In both ways they manage to absolutely enhance the flavour, and their acidity is always appreciated as a counterbalance , or just to kick you salivary glands into overdrive!

  • Red onions
  • White vinegar
  • Peppercorns(optional)
  • Garlic(optional)
  • Salt and Sugar

Steps

    Preparations:

  1. Slice the onions in half from the stem, then into as thing slices as you like
  2. If using garlic slice it into thin slices as well

    Brine:

  1. To a small saucepan add:
    • vinegar
    • peppercorns if using
    • garlic if using
    • salt and sugar
  2. Bring to simmer over medium heat, stirring ocassionally
  3. Remove the pan from heat once sugar and salt dissolves

    Pickle the onions:

  1. Add your sliced onions into mason jar
  2. Pour the brine into mason jar over the onions, making sure they are covered in brine completely
  3. After 10-15 minutes secure with lid
  4. Leave until cool, store in fridge for up to 1 month
